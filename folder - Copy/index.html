Cardio Monitoring

The process of tracking the heart's electrical activity and other vital signs. This can be done using a variety of devices, such as an electrocardiogram (EKG), a heart rate monitor, or a blood pressure cuff. Cardio monitoring is often used to diagnose and manage heart conditions, as well as to track the effectiveness of treatment.

Medical Treatment

The use of medical interventions to diagnose, prevent, or treat disease. Medical treatments can include medications, surgery, radiation therapy, and other procedures. The type of medical treatment that is used will depend on the specific condition that is being treated.

Emergency Help

The care that is provided to people who are experiencing a sudden, serious health event. Emergency health care can include cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR), first aid, and transport to a hospital. The goal of emergency health care is to stabilize the patient's condition and prevent further harm.

First Aid

It is the immediate care that is given to someone who is injured or ill. First aid can be provided by anyone, regardless of their medical training. The goal of first aid is to stabilize the patient's condition and prevent further harm until professional medical help arrives.
